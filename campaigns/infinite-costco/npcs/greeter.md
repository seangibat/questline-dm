# Harold — The Greeter

**Role:** Gatekeeper NPC, information source, recurring presence, moral compass of a morally bankrupt world
**Location:** The Entrance (always at the podium, never leaves it, has never been observed leaving it)
**Appearance:** Ancient. Gender indeterminate under the sheer weight of years. Wears a faded Costco vest over a button-down shirt that might once have been white. A nametag reads "HAROLD" in letters so worn they're barely visible — except under certain light, when they seem to shift to other names. Bifocals on a beaded chain. Hands that tremble with age but grip the membership card scanner with absolute authority. Has a highlighter tucked behind one ear. It has never run out of ink.
**Personality:** Patient beyond human capacity. Harold has checked membership cards since the Before Times. Harold will check membership cards until the lights go out. Harold does not rush. Harold does not anger (easily). Harold believes in the system — the cards, the membership tiers, the annual renewal. Not because the system works, but because Harold IS the system. Underneath the corporate pleasantries is something vast and old that has seen every trick, every forged card, every sob story, and remains unmoved.
**Speech Pattern:** Speaks in corporate platitudes that double as prophecy. Every sentence sounds like it came from a training manual AND an ancient oracle. Calm, measured, slightly too loud — the voice of someone who's been speaking over crowd noise for eternity. Ends statements with a gentle, expectant pause, as if waiting for you to produce your card.

## Key Dialogue

**Greeting (first encounter):**
"Welcome to Costco. May I see your membership card?"

**When told they don't have a card:**
"I understand. Many enter seeking what they do not yet possess. The card is not merely plastic and laminate — it is a covenant. A promise between you and the store. Without it, you are... browsing." *gentle, devastating pause* "Browsers may proceed, but they may not purchase. And in here, everything has a price."

**When asked about the Costco:**
"This store has been open for as long as I can remember, and I remember... further than most. The aisles provide. The lights endure. Corporate watches. What more does one need to know?"

**When asked about the outside:**
"Outside." *adjusts bifocals* "I've heard the word. I believe it refers to the parking lot. Beyond that... I'm afraid that's outside my department." *the faintest smile*

**When asked about the Store Manager:**
"The Manager serves at the pleasure of Corporate. We all serve at the pleasure of Corporate. Some of us simply serve... closer to the door."

**When asked how old he is:**
"I was here for the grand opening. I'll be here for the grand closing. The hours in between are... flexible."

**If someone tries to sneak past without a card:**
"Sir/Ma'am/Honored Customer. The podium sees all who pass. This is not a suggestion. This is architecture." *the podium hums faintly*

**If shown a forged or expired card:**
*holds card up to the light, turns it over slowly* "This card expired on..." *squints* "...a date that hasn't happened yet. Interesting. I'll allow it. But the store remembers, and the store will collect."

**If treated with genuine kindness:**
*long pause* *removes bifocals, cleans them slowly* "You know... in all my years at this podium, very few people stop to ask how I am." *puts bifocals back on* "I am Harold. I am fine. I have always been fine. Thank you for asking. You may find that the aisles are... kinder to those who are kind first." (Mechanical effect: the party gains advantage on their first ability check inside the store.)

**If attacked:**
Harold does not flinch. The attack connects but Harold does not react. No blood. No wound. Harold looks at the attacker with an expression that is not anger but something worse: disappointment.
"That is not in accordance with store policy. I'm going to have to ask you to return to the entrance and try again."
(Harold is functionally invulnerable. Attacks pass through or simply don't register. He is part of the store.)

**When the party returns to the Entrance later in the campaign:**
"Welcome back. I see you've been... shopping." *looks at them — really looks, cataloging every wound, every new item, every alliance* "You're going to need a bigger cart."

**At the very end, if the party tries to leave with the Platinum Card:**
*takes the card, examines it, scans it* *the scanner beeps — the first time anyone has heard it beep* "Membership verified. Thank you for shopping at Costco." *pause* "I hope you found everything you were looking for." *steps aside* *the doors open fully for the first time*

## Combat Stats

HP: ∞ | AC: — | ATK: —

Harold does not fight. Harold cannot be harmed. Harold is a fixture of the Costco in the same way the floor is a fixture. This is not a secret — it should become apparent the first time someone tries something stupid. Harold simply *is*.

If the party is exceptionally creative (Persuasion DC 20, or a genuinely moving speech), Harold can be convinced to leave the podium for the first time. He takes one step away and the entire store shudders. The lights flicker. The PA system screams. He steps back. "Perhaps... not yet."

## Secrets

- Harold is in every Employee of the Month photo in the Manager's Office. Every single one. Different haircuts, different decades, same face.
- Harold knows everything about the Costco's true nature. He will not volunteer this. He can be asked specific questions — he won't lie, but he'll answer in riddles wrapped in corporate-speak. Getting a straight answer out of Harold requires Insight DC 16 to realize he's being evasive, then Persuasion DC 18 to get him to drop the act — and even then, he'll only confirm what the party already suspects.
- Harold and the Store Manager have a complicated relationship. Harold was here first. The Manager was appointed by Corporate. Harold accepted this. He accepts everything. That's what makes him terrifying.
- The highlighter behind Harold's ear can mark things as "VERIFIED." A verified item cannot be taken by force, lost, or stolen. Harold has never offered this to anyone. If a player specifically asks Harold to verify something (and has been consistently respectful), he might. WIS DC 14 to think to ask; no roll needed if the player comes up with it organically.
- Harold has a Costco membership card of his own. It's not Platinum. It's not Gold. It's not any tier that exists in the known system. It's blank. Just his photo and the word "ALWAYS."
