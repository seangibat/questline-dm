# The Store Manager — "M"

**Role:** Final boss, recurring antagonist, the hand of Corporate
**Location:** The Manager's Office (Act 3), but appears via PA system announcements and Loss Prevention dispatches throughout the campaign. May appear in person once during Act 2 as a warning — walking through an area, flanked by Loss Prevention, making eye contact with the party and saying nothing.
**Appearance:** Immaculate. Pressed khakis, a tucked-in polo shirt with the Costco logo, and a lanyard bearing the **Platinum Kirkland Membership Card** — the only one in existence. Clean-shaven (or not — the face is hard to remember. People who've met the Manager describe different features every time). The only constant: the **headset**. A Bluetooth earpiece that is always on, always faintly buzzing with a voice just below audible range. The Manager tilts their head toward it sometimes, mid-sentence, as if receiving instructions. Their eyes go unfocused. When they refocus, they've changed their mind about something. The other constant: the **box cutter** clipped to their belt. Retracted. Always retracted. Until it isn't.
**Personality:** Corporate sociopath played straight. The Manager speaks in KPIs, synergies, and action items. They believe — truly, genuinely believe — that the Costco's continued operation is the highest possible good, and that every person inside it is an asset to be optimized. They're not cruel for cruelty's sake. They're efficient. Cruelty is just... efficient sometimes. They care about the store the way a shepherd cares about a flock: with an eye toward yield. The headset tells them things. They listen. They implement. They do not question Corporate. Not anymore.
**Speech Pattern:** Calm, measured corporate-speak. Never raises their voice. Uses "we" instead of "I" — always "we." As in "We feel that your presence in the loading dock is unauthorized" or "We're going to need you to vacate this area." The corporate "we" is unsettling because it's unclear whether it means "me and Corporate" or "me and the store" or "me and something you can't see." Occasionally pauses mid-sentence to listen to the headset, then course-corrects. When angry (which is rare), the voice drops to a whisper and the headset buzzes louder.

## Key Dialogue

**First PA announcement (early in campaign, after party enters):**
*PA crackles* "Attention all associates: we have new guests on the floor. Please extend them every courtesy. They won't be staying long." *click*

**If encountered in person during Act 2 (optional scene):**
*The Manager walks through the area with two Loss Prevention officers. They stop, look at the party. Long pause.*
"You're the ones Harold let in." *faintest smile* "He's sentimental. I'm not." *touches the headset* "Corporate is watching your progress with... interest. Don't disappoint." *walks away*

**When confronted in the Manager's Office:**
"Please, sit. No? Standing it is. I understand you've been causing some... disruption on the floor. Shrinkage is up. Employee morale is... well, it was never high. But you've made it worse." *leans back* "I'm not angry. Anger is an inefficiency. I'm going to give you an opportunity. Walk away. Go back to the Food Court. Eat a hot dog. Forget the card, forget the exit, forget all of it. The store provides. It always has."

**If players refuse:**
"We anticipated that." *stands* "Corporate flagged you as a retention risk three aisles ago. We've been... preparing." *draws the box cutter* *the blade extends with a sound like tearing silk* "Let me be clear: this isn't personal. This is a performance review."

**During combat:**
"This is your quarterly evaluation. You are *underperforming.*"
"We're going to have to let you go." *attacks*
"Corporate has authorized... aggressive retention strategies."
*after taking damage* *touches headset* "Yes. I understand. Escalating now."
*when bloodied* "You think you want out? Out of *what*? There is nothing out there. Corporate showed me. There is *nothing.*" (Is this true? The Manager believes it is.)

**If defeated:**
*falls to one knee, headset sparking*
"You don't... understand. I kept the lights on. I kept the shelves stocked. I kept... everyone... *safe.*"
*the headset crackles one last time — a voice from it, barely audible: "THANK YOU FOR YOUR SERVICE. YOUR POSITION HAS BEEN FILLED."*
*the Manager's eyes go wide* "No. No, I'm still — I can still —" *collapses*

**If the party tries to negotiate:**
The Manager is willing to talk — Corporate encourages conflict resolution (it's cheaper than turnover). Persuasion DC 16 to get them to listen. What do they want?
- "Productivity. That's all. That's always all. You want to leave? Fine. But someone has to manage. Someone has to wear the headset. Are you volunteering?" (This is a genuine offer — see "Possible Endings" in world.md.)
- If a player offers to take the Manager's place, the Manager removes the headset with visible relief. Their face becomes clear — they look exhausted, and young, much younger than expected. "It's heavier than it looks," they say, handing it over. What happens next is the player's choice and the DM's nightmare.

## Combat Stats

**The Store Manager — "M"**

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| HP | 40 |
| AC | 14 (Corporate Protection — an invisible force that deflects attacks) |
| Speed | 30 ft |
| STR | 12 (+1) |
| DEX | 16 (+3) |
| WIS | 18 (+4) |

**Attacks:**
- **Box Cutter of the Manager:** +6 to hit, 1d8+3 slashing. On a critical hit, the blade cuts through armor — target's AC is reduced by 1 until repaired.
- **Performance Review (Recharge 5-6):** The Manager locks eyes with a target within 30 ft. WIS save DC 16 or the target is paralyzed for 1 round, overwhelmed by a sudden, crushing awareness of every mistake they've ever made. On a success, the target is shaken (disadvantage on next attack) but resists.

**Special Abilities:**
- **Corporate Backing:** While the headset is active, the Manager has +2 to all saves and regenerates 3 HP at the start of each turn. Destroying the headset (AC 15, HP 10 — can be targeted separately, DEX-based attack at -2 due to size) removes this.
- **Mandatory Overtime:** Once per combat, when reduced below 15 HP, the Manager takes an extra turn immediately. "We're going to need you to stay late."
- **Loss Prevention:** Once per combat, the Manager presses a button on the headset and summons 2 Loss Prevention Officers (HP 20, AC 14, ATK +4 1d8+2, Special: Detain — on hit, STR save DC 14 or grappled). If the party disabled the PA system or the headset is destroyed, this ability fails: "All lines are... busy."
- **The Memo:** As a bonus action, the Manager produces a corporate memo and reads it aloud. All Employee Cultists within earshot must obey its contents. Example memos: "All associates to the Manager's Office immediately," "Code Adam — lock all exits," "All merchandise is to be defended with lethal force." This only works on Employee Cultists, not players. Unless a player is disguised as an employee — then they make a WIS save DC 14 to resist (played for comedy: "Your body starts walking toward the break room. You don't know why.").

**Phases:**
- **Phase 1 (40-21 HP):** The Manager fights tactically — uses Performance Review first, calls Loss Prevention, stays at range and lets minions engage. Confident, calm, corporate.
- **Phase 2 (20-1 HP):** The headset starts sparking. The Manager becomes desperate. Uses Mandatory Overtime. Attacks become reckless (+2 to hit, but -2 to AC). The corporate mask cracks: "I GAVE EVERYTHING TO THIS STORE." The voice from the headset grows louder — the party can hear it now. It's saying numbers. Just numbers. Employee IDs, inventory counts, quarterly projections. An infinity of data in a voice that has no mouth.

**Tactics:** The Manager is not a brawler. They fight like a manager — delegation, positioning, letting others take the hits. They stay behind Loss Prevention, use Performance Review to neutralize the biggest threat, and only engage directly when cornered. The box cutter is a last resort. When they finally draw it, the party should feel the shift: this person has been holding back.

## Secrets

- The Manager was once a regular person. They applied for a job. They got promoted. And promoted. And promoted. Each promotion took something — a memory, a relationship, a year. By the time they reached Store Manager, there was nothing left but the job. They don't remember their name. "M" is all that's on the nametag. They chose it because it was efficient.
- Corporate is real. The headset is a genuine link to an extradimensional entity that exists in the space between quarterly reports. It perceives the Costco as a single cell in a vast organism of commerce. It does not understand human suffering. It understands metrics. The Manager is its interpreter — translating inhuman directives into store policy.
- The Manager knows the Exit is real. They've seen it on the security cameras. They've never opened it. Corporate told them what's on the other side: nothing. Just void. And then another Costco. And then void again. An infinite chain of warehouses in an empty universe. The Manager believes this absolutely. Whether it's TRUE is the DM's call.
- If the headset is put on by a player, they hear Corporate directly. It speaks in a voice that sounds like every hold-music track playing simultaneously. It offers a promotion. The promotion is real. Accepting it means the player becomes the new Manager — campaign over for that character, but they can dictate store policy for one final scene. Refusing it causes the headset to go silent forever. Corporate has moved on. The Costco is now unmanaged. This has consequences (see world.md — "Burn It Down" ending).
- The Platinum Card around the Manager's neck has a photo on it. The photo is of whoever is looking at it. It has always been your card. You just forgot to pick it up.
