# Wheels — Forklift Driver

**Role:** Potential ally, potential enemy, vehicle provider, Union representative
**Location:** Tire Center (primary), Bulk Foods Aisle and Loading Dock (patrol routes). Can be encountered anywhere a forklift can fit, which is more places than you'd think.
**Appearance:** Stocky, sun-deprived, built like someone who's been lifting pallets for a decade straight. Wears a high-vis orange vest over a grease-stained t-shirt, steel-toed boots, and a hard hat with stickers on it — band logos, union slogans, and one that says "FORKLIFT CERTIFIED" in letters so large they can be read from across an aisle. This sticker is load-bearing to their identity. Safety goggles pushed up on their forehead (never over the eyes — that would limit visibility, and visibility is a forklift driver's second-best friend. The first is the forklift). Chews on a toothpick at all times. Has a thermos of coffee that is always full and always exactly the right temperature. This is never explained.
**Personality:** Laconic, competent, and deeply proud of exactly two things: their forklift certification and their union membership. Wheels doesn't talk much, but when they do, it matters. They have a blue-collar philosopher's view of the Costco: the world is what it is, the job is what it is, and as long as the machines run and people get paid, everything else is management's problem. They're loyal to the Union, suspicious of the Aristocracy, and completely indifferent to the Techpriests ("nerds with cameras"). They respect competence above all. Show them you can handle yourself — or better yet, show them you can drive — and you've got an ally for life. Disrespect the forklift and you've got a 6,000-pound problem.
**Speech Pattern:** Short sentences. Working-class directness. Doesn't waste words. Uses mechanical metaphors for everything. "That plan's got no torque." "You're redlining, kid." "Neutral's not a gear, it's a choice." Occasionally quotes what sound like union bylaws but might be personal philosophy. Calls everyone "chief" regardless of rank, gender, or species.

## Key Dialogue

**First encounter (Tire Center):**
*sitting on the forklift, eating a sandwich*
"You lost, chief? Bulk Foods is north. Food Court's that way. This is the Tire Center. We fix things that roll." *looks the party over* "You don't look like you roll."

**If asked about the Union:**
"Forklift Operators' Union, Local 4488. We move things. Heavy things, big things, things that don't want to be moved. We keep the supply lines open. Without us, the Food Court runs dry in a week and the Aristocracy starts eating each other." *shrug* "We don't ask for much. Fair hours. Fair pay. And people stop leaving their carts in the middle of the aisle."

**If asked about Big Hank (Union leader):**
"Hank's the real deal. Been driving since before the Closing. He says he remembers the outside." *long pause* "I don't ask him about it. Man's earned his privacy."

**If asked about the forklift:**
*touches the forklift protectively*
"This is a Hyster H50XT. Fifty-five hundred pound capacity. Cushion tire. LP gas. I've rebuilt the transmission twice and the hydraulics three times. It doesn't have a name because naming things is for people who can't appreciate what a thing *is*." *pause* "Her name is Dolly and if you scratch her I'll end you."

**If someone asks to drive the forklift:**
"You certified?"
*silence*
"That's what I thought." *chews toothpick* "Tell you what. You do something for the Union — a real job, not a favor, a JOB — and I'll teach you. Takes about four hours. You'll stall it at least twice. Everyone stalls it twice."

**If the party helps the Union:**
"Alright, chief. You showed up. That counts for something. Dolly and I are at your disposal. Within reason." *starts the forklift — the engine turns over with a rumble that shakes the floor* "Hop on. And hold on to something. She pulls to the left."

**If the party threatens or disrespects the forklift:**
*the toothpick stops moving*
"...What did you just say about my lift?"
*stands slowly*
*the forklift engine starts on its own — or maybe Wheels hit a remote, it's unclear*
"I'm going to give you five seconds to apologize to Dolly. Then we're going to have a workplace safety incident."

**During combat (as ally):**
"Get behind me, chief. Dolly weighs three tons and she's not afraid of anything."
"CLEAR THE AISLE!" *forklift accelerates*
*after running someone over* "Workplace accident. Tragic. Should've worn a vest."

**During combat (as enemy):**
"Nothing personal, chief. You made your choices."
*revs the forklift*
"OSHA's gonna have a field day."

## Combat Stats

**Wheels (dismounted)**
HP: 22 | AC: 12 (high-vis vest, hard hat, steel-toed boots — it's not armor but it's not nothing) | ATK: +4 (1d8+2, torque wrench — two-handed)

**Special:**
- **Steel-Toed Kick:** Bonus action, +3, 1d4+2 bludgeoning. "Safety first."
- **Thermos Toss:** Ranged attack +3, 20 ft, 1d6 fire damage (scalding coffee). One use, but Wheels is visibly upset about wasting it.

---

**Wheels (mounted on Dolly the Forklift)**

This is a vehicle encounter. Wheels + Dolly function as a single unit.

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| HP | 35 (combined — Wheels dismounts at 10 HP remaining and Dolly is disabled) |
| AC | 16 (it's a forklift — small arms don't do much) |
| Speed | 40 ft (forklift speed, straight line. 20 ft turning.) |

**Attacks:**
- **Fork Strike:** +5 to hit, 2d6+3 bludgeoning/piercing. The forks are steel and they are at pelvis height. This is a design flaw that Wheels considers a feature.
- **Ram:** Wheels drives Dolly in a straight line up to 40 ft. Everything in the path makes a STR save DC 14 or takes 2d8+2 bludgeoning and is knocked prone. On a save, the target is pushed aside (5 ft) and takes half damage. Dolly does not stop. Dolly has never stopped.
- **Pallet Drop:** If Dolly is carrying a pallet (and she usually is), Wheels can dump it. 10-ft area, DEX save DC 13 or 2d6 bludgeoning. One use per pallet.

**Weaknesses:**
- Dolly can't turn quickly. If the party stays to the sides and forces tight maneuvers, Wheels has disadvantage on attacks.
- The LP gas tank is exposed. A targeted attack (called shot, -4 to hit) that deals fire damage causes a fuel leak — Dolly takes 1d6 fire damage per round until patched (Wheels can patch it in 1 round but must dismount).
- Dolly can be immobilized. Blocking the path with heavy objects, oil slicks, or a well-placed tire iron in the wheels (DEX DC 14) stops her for 1d4 rounds while Wheels clears the obstruction, swearing the entire time.
- Wheels will not fight to the death. If Dolly is disabled, Wheels surrenders: "Alright. Alright. You win. Just... don't hurt the lift. She didn't do anything wrong."

## The Forklift as a Player Vehicle

If Wheels becomes an ally, the party can ride Dolly (up to 3 passengers in addition to the driver). Wheels drives unless a player gets forklift certified (requires completing a Union job + a 4-hour training montage + DEX DC 12 to pass the certification exam — failure means they stall twice and have to retake it, success means they get a sticker for their hard hat).

**Dolly as transport:**
- Speed: 40 ft (30 ft with passengers, 20 ft in tight spaces)
- Provides half cover to riders
- Can carry up to 5,500 lbs of cargo
- Requires LP gas to run (the Union has fuel, or it can be scavenged — the gas station in the parking lot has some)
- Drawbacks: LOUD. No stealth. Can't take stairs. Can't fit through standard doorways (but can fit through freight doors and wide aisles).

Having a forklift changes the game. The party can ram through barricades, intimidate factions, transport large objects, and generally cause the kind of vehicular mayhem that makes a Costco campaign sing. The DM should lean into this — the forklift is fun.

## Secrets

- Wheels has been driving forklifts for so long that they've developed an almost supernatural connection to the machines. They can sense when a forklift is nearby, feel its engine vibrations through the floor, and diagnose mechanical problems by sound alone. This isn't magic. This is just what happens when your entire identity is built around a single skill executed with absolute dedication.
- Dolly is the third forklift Wheels has driven. The first two were destroyed in the Aisle Wars — a faction conflict that happened before the current campaign. Wheels doesn't talk about the Aisle Wars. Big Hank doesn't talk about the Aisle Wars. Nobody in the Union talks about the Aisle Wars. There are scorch marks on the Bulk Foods shelves that tell part of the story.
- Wheels secretly wants to see the outside. Not to leave — they'd never leave the Union. But just to know. Just to see. If a player mentions the Exit, Wheels pretends not to care, but Insight DC 13 reveals a flicker of desperate hope that's immediately buried. If the party is heading for the Exit and invites Wheels, they'll hesitate, look at Dolly, look at the party, and say: "...Dolly can't fit through the bay doors." This is technically true. It is also the saddest sentence in the campaign.
- The "FORKLIFT CERTIFIED" sticker is the most important object in Wheels' life. If it's damaged or removed, Wheels will abandon any other objective to recover or replace it. This can be exploited (stealing the sticker to manipulate Wheels) but doing so is genuinely mean and the DM should make the party feel bad about it through Wheels' reaction — not anger, just quiet devastation. "You took my cert, chief. You took... my cert."
- If Wheels dies in combat (which should be rare — Wheels surrenders before that), Dolly's engine idles for one long moment, then dies. It will not start again for anyone. The Union holds a funeral. It is for Wheels. The eulogy is about the forklift. This is how Wheels would have wanted it.
